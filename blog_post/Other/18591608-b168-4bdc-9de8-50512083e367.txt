New Jersey has issued a public warning about potential measles exposures at Newark Liberty International Airport, prompting contact tracing efforts from local health officials.

Measles is highly contagious airborne virus that can remain in the air for up to two hours after an infected person was present. According to county health officials, symptoms typically appear 10-12 days after exposure but can manifest within a week or up to three weeks later.

The Department of Health is collaborating with local authorities to inform individuals who may have been exposed and encourage vaccination. This timely warning is crucial as the virus has been reported in multiple cases in New Jersey, and its transmission can spread quickly through close contact.

By staying informed about measles exposure at Newark Liberty International Airport, travelers can take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their loved ones from this potentially life-threatening disease. Stay up-to-date with health officials and plan accordingly to minimize the risk of infection.
