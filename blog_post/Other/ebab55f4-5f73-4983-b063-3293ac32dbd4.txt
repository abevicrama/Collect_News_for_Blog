The long-standing drought in California is a pressing issue for states along the Colorado River, which supplies over 40 million people with water. The situation is further complicated by the region's reliance on Colorado River water, making it a critical component of their economic and environmental health. To address this issue, Utah State Senator Ralph O'Hara has proposed an innovative idea: exchanging shares of water in the Colorado Basin for access to the river.

This concept leverages blockchain technology to facilitate secure and transparent exchange of water rights. By using smart contracts, states can guarantee a fair allocation of their share and reduce disputes over water usage. Additionally, this approach aligns with the increasing adoption of data-driven decision making, which has improved water management across various industries.
