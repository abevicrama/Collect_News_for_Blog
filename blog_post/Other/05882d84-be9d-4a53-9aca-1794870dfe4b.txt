Ubisoft's decision to shut down its servers and marketplace for Rainbow Six Siege has sent shockwaves through the gaming community, raising questions about the security measures in place to protect user accounts. As a result of a major hack, Ubisoft claims it had been compromised, leading to the shutdown of servers and a temporary halt on new game sales.

The impact is significant, with over 15 million Rainbow Six Siege credits being suspended due to the issue. For context, that's approximately $13.33 million in cash value, equivalent to two billion R6 credits. Given the massive financial implications, it's no surprise that Ubisoft decided not to punish players for spending their earned credits.
